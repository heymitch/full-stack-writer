# Newsletter Writer

Draft a complete, voice-matched newsletter from topic to final subject line. Uses the Full Stack Writer framework.

## Setup

1. Read `training/voice/voice-template.md` — REQUIRED before any writing
2. Check `.coworker/index.md` for niche, audience, and content pillars
3. Read `references/newsletter-formats.md` and `references/subject-line-patterns.md`

## Editorial Assessment (Do This First)

Before writing a single word, get 4 answers. Pull from context if available. Ask if not.

**1. Why now?**
What makes this topic timely? Recent event, trend, personal experience, conversation? If the answer is "nothing in particular," push for specificity. Timeless topics still need a present-tense reason to exist.

**2. What's the one-sentence argument?**
Not the topic. The take. "Why consistency beats talent in audience building" is a topic. "Most writers never build an audience because they're optimizing for quality before they've earned the right to" is a take. Push until you have a take.

**3. Who is this for?**
The person who [has this problem] but hasn't [tried this approach] yet. Specific. Not "writers." "Freelance writers who post on LinkedIn but feel like they're talking to nobody."

**4. What does the reader DO after reading?**
Reply and share something? Try a specific technique today? Change a belief? There must be an action. If there's no clear action, the newsletter has no landing point.

If you can't get all 4 answers, stop. Surface-level answers produce pretty essays that circle concepts without landing.

## Format Selection

Based on the topic and niche, recommend a format:

- **Original Thinking** — for slow-moving niches, strong opinions, deep expertise. Subscriber promise: accelerates expertise.
- **Curation** — for fast-moving niches, time-saving aggregation. Subscriber promise: saves time.
- **Hybrid** — one original section + curated resources. Works when the writer has a take but also has great sources.

Use templates from `references/newsletter-formats.md`.

## Draft Process

### Step 1: Outline
Show the skeleton before writing the full draft. Get approval.

For Original Thinking:
- Big idea / the reframe
- Main point 1 (mistake + fix + example)
- Main point 2
- Main point 3
- Conclusion / action

For Curation:
- Intro hook
- Resource list (4-6 items with descriptions)
- Tip of the week (expanded breakdown)
- Industry news (3 items with hot takes)
- CTA / sign-off

### Step 2: Draft in Voice

Write the full draft using the voice template. Do not write generic newsletter prose. Key signals from Full Stack Writer framework:
- Newsletter is about DOING, not "learning." Every section should help the reader accomplish something.
- Specificity is the advantage. Vague = untrustworthy.
- Write to one person solving one problem—the internet will surface it to everyone with the same problem.
- Short paragraphs. White space. No walls of text.

### Step 3: Subject Lines

Generate 4 subject lines using the 4 hook types from `references/subject-line-patterns.md`:
- Subject Line 1 (Value for Time): [subject] | Subtext: [whisper]
- Subject Line 2 (Value for Cost): [subject] | Subtext: [whisper]
- Subject Line 3 (Effortless Solution): [subject] | Subtext: [whisper]
- Subject Line 4 (Instant Outcome): [subject] | Subtext: [whisper]

All subject lines: sentence case, 5-15 words, no Title Case.

## Save Output

Save draft to `output/drafts/[YYYY-MM-DD]-[slug].md`
Update `.coworker/index.md` with published topic + date
