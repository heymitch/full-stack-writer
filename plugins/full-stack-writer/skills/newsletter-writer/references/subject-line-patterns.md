# Subject Line Patterns

## The 4 Hooks That Always Work

Every subject line that drives opens is promising exactly one of these four things:

1. **Value for minimal time** — "Your entire [topic] blueprint in 1 email"
2. **Value for minimal cost** — "Here's our entire [process]. Steal it."
3. **Solve problem with minimal effort** — "[Problem]? Just do [X]."
4. **Unlock desirable outcome, instantly** — "[N] keys to [outcome] right now"

Pick one hook. Write to it. Everything else is execution.

---

## The 6 Pieces of a Great Subject Line

| Piece | What it does | Example |
|---|---|---|
| HOW MANY | Sets quantity expectations | "7 reasons", "3 steps", "11 mistakes" |
| THE WHO | Specifies the reader | "for freelancers", "for founders", "for coaches" |
| THE WHAT | Names the tangible thing | "tips", "frameworks", "templates", "case studies" |
| THE WHY | States the outcome | "to land your first client", "to grow to 10k" |
| TWIST THE KNIFE | Adds a secondary outcome or stakes | "and never feel stuck again" |
| WHISPER (preview text) | The "here's what's inside" follow-up | "From someone who's done it 500 times." |

You don't need all 6 in every subject line. Get as many as fit without exceeding the word limit.

---

## The Rules

**Rule 1: 5-15 words max.** If it gets cut off before the hook, no one clicks. Less is almost always more—but don't cut words if it costs the meaning.

**Rule 2: Sentence case, not Title Case.** "3 mistakes keeping you from your first $5,000" outperforms "3 Mistakes Keeping You From Your First $5,000." Sentence case reads like a friend, not a headline.

**Rule 3: Use 1 of the 4 hooks.** No hook = no reason to click.

**Rule 4: Use visceral language when appropriate.** OUCH! / FREE! / Steal this. / (I can't believe I'm sharing this.) Small additions that spike emotional response.

**Rule 5: Name & Claim.** Giving the framework a name makes the email feel like a tangible asset.
- Generic: "3 ways to make more money as a life coach"
- Named: "Life Coach Money Manual: 3 ways to 10x your monthly earnings"

**Rule 6: Be super specific.** "How to write better" = ignored. "How to cut your newsletter draft time in half (without losing your voice)" = opened.

---

## The 4 Whisper Types (Preview Text)

The preview text is the grey sentence next to the subject line. It whispers what's inside. Use one of these:

- **Trust whisper** — "Written by someone who's done this 500 times."
- **Obstacle whisper** — "Without spending a dollar on ads."
- **Benefit whisper** — "And how to fix it starting today."
- **Outcome whisper** — "And start earning $10k/month from your writing."

---

## The 4 Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Too long** — cut off before the hook = no click
2. **Formal tone** — reads like corporate email, not a friend
3. **Weak or no hook** — gives no reason to open
4. **Ignoring preview text** — wasted reinforcement opportunity

---

## A/B Testing Subject Lines

The secret: don't A/B test word order. Test completely different ideas.

Weak test: "3 mistakes ghostwriters make" vs. "Dear Ghostwriter: stop making these 3 mistakes"
Strong test: "3 mistakes ghostwriters make" vs. "How to make an extra $5,000 this month as a ghostwriter"

Test different hooks—the data tells you which ideas your readers actually care about, not just which phrasing they prefer.
