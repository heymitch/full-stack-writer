# Email Sequence Templates

## Core Principles (Apply to All Sequences)

**What subscribers are really saying when they sign up:** "I agree to receive emails from you."
That's it. Your job is to turn that passive agreement into active anticipation.

**The 3 mistakes that kill sequences:**
1. Not enough emails — one welcome email is not a sequence
2. Making it about you — every story, every insight must serve the reader's goals
3. Selling too early — educate before you sell; people only buy when they trust you can solve their problem

**The anatomy that works:** Trust → Expertise → Soft pitch. In that order, always.

---

## Sequence 1: Newsletter Welcome Sequence (4 emails)

**Purpose:** Turn a new subscriber into an engaged reader who trusts you and knows what to expect.

**Timing:** Email immediately, then 24-hour gaps.

---

### Email 1: "What To Expect"

**Goal:** Set expectations so they actually open future emails.

**Template:**
```
Hey!

And welcome to {newsletter name}!

I'm on a mission to help {group of people} {overcome niche problem} and {unlock desirable outcome}.

I'm in the business of helping you become a {desired identity}.

As a {newsletter name} subscriber, you'll receive {tangible value #1}, {tangible value #2}, and {tangible value #3} every {specific weekday}.

My goal with each email is to make you think, "Why is this free?"

Because that's what people on a mission do.

It sounds like each email is a mini-course in {your topic}. That's right.

I could charge hundreds of dollars for the insight I pack into these newsletters.

But I want to give it all to you for free—because I'm committed to helping more {group} {unlock desirable outcome}, and I want my emails to be so valuable, you'll read them all.

Talk next {weekday},
{Your name}

P.S. While you wait for my next email, I've put together a bonus resource to help you {solve specific problem}. Reply with the word "{keyword}" and I'll send it over.
```

---

### Email 2: "The Greatest Hits"

**Goal:** Introduce your best content immediately—don't make them wait weeks to see what you can do.

**Template:**
```
Hey!

Today I'd like to share my best resources to help you master the fundamentals of {skill/niche}:
- {Fundamental topic 1}
- {Fundamental topic 2}
- {Fundamental topic 3}

Here's the thing: the hardest part of {unlocking desired outcome} is mastering these fundamentals.

Once you {overcome common misconception}, you'll start to {experience benefit}.

Here are the resources to start going down the rabbit hole:

1. {Resource name} ({time to consume}): {2-3 sentences selling why this resource matters}

2. {Resource name} ({time to consume}): {2-3 sentences}

3. {Resource name} ({time to consume}): {2-3 sentences}

But don't forget: you won't {unlock outcome} just by consuming these.

Keep {doing the key activity}, keep reading, and put it into practice.

1 month from now, you'll look back and see how much you've grown.

Chat soon,
{Your name}
```

---

### Email 3: "The Biggest Mistake"

**Goal:** Demonstrate expertise by naming the #1 mistake in your niche—and showing you know the fix.

**Template:**
```
Hey!

Have you ever heard this advice?

"{Common piece of advice in your niche that's actually wrong or incomplete}"

That's cool advice, but here's the problem: {explain why this advice fails or is misunderstood}.

It's vague and confusing, and therefore, unhelpful.

Here's what actually works instead:

{Your specific, contrarian principle}

{Explain the principle in 3-5 sentences with concrete specificity}

For example: {real-world example that makes it tangible}

See the difference?

{1 sentence summarizing the reframe}

Keep practicing,
{Your name}

P.S. {Optional: tease next email or soft mention of a paid offer}
```

---

### Email 4: "The Origin Story"

**Goal:** Build human connection. Soft-introduce your paid offer.

**Template:**
```
Hey!

Before I send you into my regular email rhythm, I wanted to share something personal.

{2-3 sentences: where you were before—specific struggle, specific moment of frustration}

{2-3 sentences: what changed—the insight, the turning point, the thing you discovered}

{2-3 sentences: where you are now—what became possible}

I share this because I know {group} face the same struggle I did. And I genuinely want to see you get to the other side.

That's why I built {newsletter name}—and why I also created {paid product/offer}. {1 sentence: what it is and who it's for}.

If you're at the stage where {relevant circumstance}, it might be worth checking out: {link}

No pressure. The free emails are always going to be packed with value either way.

Talk soon,
{Your name}
```

## Sequence 2: Pre-Launch Sequence (10 days)

**Purpose:** Build anticipation before a product launch. Prime buyers.

**Structure:**
- Days 1-3: Problem awareness — make them feel the pain of not having the solution
- Days 4-6: Solution teasing — introduce the concept, not the product
- Days 7-8: Social proof — stories, results, testimonials
- Days 9-10: The offer — reveal the product with clear details and deadline

**Key principle:** Don't reveal the product until Day 9. Everything before is about deepening the problem → making the solution feel necessary.

---

## Sequence 3: Product Launch Sequence (10 days)

**Purpose:** Drive purchases during an open cart.

**Structure:**
- Day 1 (Launch day): Full reveal — product, price, promise, deadline
- Days 2-4: Objection handling — one objection per email
- Days 5-7: Social proof and story emails
- Days 8-9: Urgency escalation — cart closing soon
- Day 10 (Close): Final call — cart closes tonight, last chance CTA

**Rules:**
- Every email stands alone — don't assume they read the previous one
- State the deadline clearly in every launch email
- The close email should be short — urgency, final benefit, link

---

## Sequence 4: Abandoned Cart (3-4 emails)

**Purpose:** Re-engage someone who showed intent but didn't buy.

**Email 1 (1 hour after abandon):** "Did something go wrong?" — simple, no pressure, offer help
**Email 2 (24 hours):** Restate the core benefit, address the most common objection
**Email 3 (48 hours):** Social proof — testimonial or result from someone similar
**Email 4 (72 hours):** Urgency — if there's a real deadline, state it clearly and close

---

## Sequence 5: Book-a-Call (5-7 emails)

**Purpose:** Move warm leads to a sales conversation.

**Structure:**
- Email 1: The "are you the right fit?" qualifier — describe the ideal client specifically
- Emails 2-4: Case studies / transformation stories (one per email)
- Email 5: Address the top objection directly
- Email 6-7: Direct CTA — book a call. Simple, clear, no fluff.

**Rule:** Don't pitch the call in email 1. Earn the right first.
