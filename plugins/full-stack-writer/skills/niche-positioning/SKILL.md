# Niche Positioning

Help a writer find their specific angle, name their newsletter, and write a description that attracts exactly the right subscribers.

## Setup

1. Check `.coworker/index.md` for any existing niche or positioning work
2. Read `references/positioning-frameworks.md`
3. Voice template not required here — this is strategic, not creative writing

## Determine What's Needed

Ask the writer which of these they need:

1. **Pick a niche** — they haven't settled on a topic yet
2. **Niche down further** — they have a broad topic but it's still too general
3. **Name the newsletter** — topic is clear, need a name
4. **Write the description / about section** — name exists, need the positioning copy
5. **Full positioning sprint** — all of the above from scratch

## Process by Mode

---

### Mode 1: Pick or Niche Down a Topic

**Step 1: Establish current thinking**
Ask: What broad topic are you writing about? What do you know a lot about? What could you write about for 3 years without running out of ideas?

**Step 2: Apply the DOING reframe**
Take their topic and convert it from "learning" language to "doing" language. Show the difference. Get their reaction.

**Step 3: Top 10 Problems / Top 10 Outcomes exercise**
Walk them through it:
- List 10 biggest problems their reader faces in this niche
- List 10 most desirable outcomes their reader wants to achieve

Identify the intersection: problems they know how to solve + outcomes they're excited to help with.

**Step 4: Apply the 9 Niche-Down Dimensions**
Take their topic and generate 5-7 specific variations using different dimensions from the frameworks. Present as options, not prescriptions. Let them react.

Ask: Which one makes you feel "that's exactly who I want to serve"?

**Step 5: Competitive check**
Ask if they know who else is writing about this. If so, identify the gap. If not, proceed.

---

### Mode 2: Name the Newsletter

Collect:
- Their niche-down (specific reader + specific outcome)
- The tone they want (professional? punchy? authoritative? warm?)
- Any names they've already considered

Generate 10 name options across these categories:
- 3 outcome-focused names (name the result)
- 3 category-creating names (coin a new term)
- 3 reader-identity names (make the reader feel like they belong)
- 1 wildcard (unexpected but memorable)

For each, include a 1-sentence rationale.

Narrow to top 3 with the writer's input. Recommend the strongest one and explain why.

---

### Mode 3: Newsletter Description / About Section

Collect:
- Newsletter name (confirmed)
- The specific reader (detailed)
- The main problem solved
- The outcome delivered
- Publishing frequency

Write 3 description versions:
- **Short (1-liner):** For social bios, signup pages — one sentence, max 20 words
- **Medium (3-5 sentences):** For the newsletter's About section on Substack/Beehiiv
- **Long (1 paragraph):** For the full About page or landing page copy

Each version must pass the stranger test: someone who has never heard of the newsletter understands immediately who it's for and what they'll get.

---

## Output Format

Present options clearly labeled. Include a recommendation.

After each exercise, ask: "Does this feel right, or do we need to push further?"

Positioning is iterative. Better to sharpen 3 times than ship something generic.

## Save Output

Save final positioning to `output/positioning.md`
Update `.coworker/index.md` with confirmed niche, newsletter name, and 1-liner.
