# Platform Rules for Repurposing

## The Core Principle

Your newsletter is not separate from your social content. It's the expanded, more valuable version of it. The relationship:

- **Social** → attracts new readers, proves what resonates, drives subscriptions
- **Newsletter** → rewards subscribers with depth, converts readers into buyers
- **Repurposing** → squeezes every piece of content until all the juice is out

Inside every newsletter are 10-30 tweets, 5-10 LinkedIn posts, 1-2 threads, and dozens of video scripts. Extract them.

---

## The Extract → Compress → Expand Method

**Step 1: Extract** — identify the standalone ideas inside the long-form piece. Each idea that can stand alone without context is extractable.

**Step 2: Compress** — reduce each extracted idea to its essence. Kill all setup. Lead with the payoff.

**Step 3: Expand** — for platforms that reward depth (LinkedIn), expand back out with story, stakes, or specificity.

## Platform-Specific Rules

### X / Twitter

**Character limit:** 280 per tweet
**Formats that work:**

**Style 1: The 2-Liner (Contrast/Tension)**
Set up a tension or reversal in two sentences.
```
I don't write to be "helpful."
I write to be so specific that the right person feels like I'm reading their mind.
```

**Style 2: Succinct Paragraph (Compressed Insight)**
One punchy paragraph that summarizes a big idea. No fluff.
```
If you want extraordinary outcomes, you have to do extraordinary things. You can't be like everyone else. You have to be "weird," and embrace not fitting in. You have to do what others won't, so you can live like they can't.
```

**Style 3: Bulleted Contrast List**
Two opposing groups separated by a blank line.
```
Writers who build audiences:
• Publish every week no matter what
• Write about one topic in ten ways
• Treat ideas like inventory

Writers who stay invisible:
• Wait for the perfect idea
• Write generically about everything
• Never press send
```

**Thread structure:** Hook tweet (the entire thesis in one tweet, no "thread" announced) → 5-12 tweets expanding each point → final tweet with CTA

### LinkedIn

**Character limit:** 3,000 characters for posts (algorithm favors 1,300-1,800)
**Line break rule:** Single sentences. Every 2-3 lines = visual break.

**5 LinkedIn Post Formats:**

**Format 1: The Contrarian Take**
Open with a belief most people hold. Flip it in the next line. Explain why. End with a principle.

**Format 2: The Story + Lesson**
3-5 sentences of specific story (exact numbers, real stakes). 1 transition sentence. 3-5 sentences of the lesson extracted from the story.

**Format 3: The Numbered List**
Hook line (not "Here are X tips"). Numbered items with bold lead word + explanation. Closing one-liner that reframes everything.

**Format 4: The Before / After**
What I used to believe: [wrong thing]
What I believe now: [better thing]
What changed: [the specific experience or insight]

**Format 5: The Observation**
Something you noticed that others haven't said out loud yet. No prescriptions needed—just sharp observation.

**LinkedIn rules:**
- No hashtags in the post body (save for comments if at all)
- End with a question to invite replies, or a strong statement to invite debate
- Never start with "I" as the first word
- Avoid corporate vocabulary: leverage, synergy, impactful, journey

---

### Substack Notes

**Length:** 1-3 sentences. That's it.
**Purpose:** Drive clicks to the full newsletter. Spark curiosity or debate.

**Format:** Take the single sharpest sentence from your newsletter. Expand it by one sentence that raises stakes or adds tension. Optional third sentence that's a question or a tease.

Example:
```
Most writers think they run out of ideas because they're not creative enough.
They actually run out because they're thinking about topics, not frameworks.
One framework I use generates 150 ideas from a single topic.
```

**What Notes are NOT:** summaries of the newsletter, promotional blurbs, teasers with "click here to read more." Notes stand alone or they don't work.

---

## Analytics-Driven Repurposing

Before repurposing blindly, check which content is already working:
- High views = broad reach, probably worth a newsletter deep-dive
- High likes = resonant concept, worth more posts in the same frame
- High comments = controversial or question-generating, worth a thread

Start repurposing what the data says works. Don't guess.
